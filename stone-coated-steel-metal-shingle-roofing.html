What is Stone Coated Steel Roofing?

Stone coated steel is a premium metal roofing material made from galvanized steel panels coated with natural stone granules. This outer layer shields the steel from UV rays, hail, and weathering while enhancing its appearance. Designed to mimic traditional shingles, tiles, or shakes, stone coated steel combines the strength and longevity of metal with the visual appeal of classic roofing styles, offering superior durability and resistance to denting, fading, and corrosion.

Benefits of Metal Roofing

  • Superior Longevity: Metal roofs typically last 40-70 years—2-3 times longer than traditional asphalt shingles—providing exceptional long-term value.
  • Extreme Weather Resistance: Engineered to withstand high winds (up to 140+ mph), heavy rain, hail, and snow with minimal damage or maintenance needs.
  • Energy Efficiency: Reflective properties that minimize heat absorption, potentially reducing cooling costs by 10-25% during hot Texas summers.
  • Fire Resistance: Non-combustible material with Class A fire rating, providing additional protection and potentially lower insurance premiums.
  • Environmental Benefits: 100% recyclable material with high recycled content, typically requires no tear-off of existing roofing, and reduces landfill waste.
  • Lightweight: Significantly lighter than tile or slate, reducing structural load and often eliminating the need for additional support.
  • Low Maintenance: Minimal upkeep requirements with simple periodic inspections and occasional cleaning to maintain optimal performance.
  • Impact Resistance: Superior resistance to damage from hail, falling branches, and debris compared to conventional roofing materials.
  • Insurance Advantages: Many insurance companies offer premium discounts for impact-resistant metal roofing due to its durability.
  • Increased Home Value: Metal roofing typically increases property value and enhances curb appeal with its modern, distinctive appearance.

Metal Roofing & Sustainability

When choosing a new roof, environmental impact is an increasingly important consideration. Metal roofing stands out as one of the most sustainable roofing options available today:

Recycled Content

Most metal roofing contains at least 25% recycled content, significantly reducing the environmental footprint compared to petroleum-based roofing products.

100% Recyclability

At the end of its long service life, metal roofing is 100% recyclable, preventing landfill waste and allowing the material to be reused in new products.

Energy Conservation

Reflective properties reduce heat island effect and lower cooling demands, potentially saving thousands of kilowatt-hours over the roof's lifespan.

Rainwater Collection Compatibility

Metal roofing is ideal for clean rainwater collection systems, an increasingly important consideration in water-conscious regions.

Solar Panel Integration

Metal roofs provide ideal platforms and greater ease of attachment for solar panel installation, supporting renewable energy adoption.

Reduced Replacement Waste

With a lifespan 4-5 times longer than conventional roofing, metal roofs significantly reduce the waste generated by frequent roof replacements.
