Commercial Roofing FAQs

How long does a commercial roof typically last in Central Texas?

Commercial roof lifespans vary by material and maintenance: TPO and EPDM membranes typically last 20-30 years, metal roofing 30-50 years, and built-up roofing 15-20 years. Central Texas weather conditions like intense UV exposure, hailstorms, and high winds can impact longevity, which is why regular maintenance is essential.

How can I tell if my commercial roof needs repair or replacement?

Signs your commercial roof needs attention include visible damage like blisters or tears in membrane roofing, standing water (ponding), interior water stains, increased energy bills, and visible light through the roof deck. We recommend professional inspections at least twice annually to catch issues before they become major problems.

Will my business need to close during commercial roof work?

In most cases, we can perform commercial roofing work while your business remains operational. Our team coordinates closely with property managers to minimize disruption, schedule noisy work during off-hours when possible, and implement safety measures to protect employees and customers during the project.

What are the most energy-efficient commercial roofing options for Central Texas?

For Central Texas buildings, "cool roof" options like white TPO, PVC membranes, and reflective metal roofing provide the best energy efficiency by reflecting sunlight and heat. These systems can reduce cooling costs by 15-30% during hot Texas summers. We can also add insulation during roof installation to further improve energy performance.

Does Verstera Roofing help with commercial insurance claims?

Yes, we provide comprehensive support for commercial roof insurance claims. Our team conducts thorough documentation of storm damage, provides detailed repair estimates, and works directly with your insurance adjuster to ensure you receive fair coverage for necessary repairs or replacement.
