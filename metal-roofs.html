Types of Metal Roofing We Offer

Standing Seam Metal

Premium concealed fastener system with vertical legs and elevated seams, offering a clean, contemporary appearance and superior weather resistance with no exposed fasteners.

Stone Coated Steel

Durable steel panels with stone coating that mimics the appearance of shingles, tile, or slate while providing the strength and longevity of metal roofing.

Metal Shingles

Interlocking metal shingles that offer the aesthetic of traditional roofing materials with enhanced durability and performance in extreme weather conditions.

Lightweight metal formed to resemble traditional clay or concrete tile, offering the aesthetic appeal of tile with reduced structural load requirements.

Specialized metal roofing systems designed for unique architectural styles or specific performance requirements, available in various profiles and finishes.

Benefits of Metal Roofing

  • Superior Longevity: Metal roofs typically last 40-70 years—2-3 times longer than traditional asphalt shingles—providing exceptional long-term value.
  • Extreme Weather Resistance: Engineered to withstand high winds (up to 140+ mph), heavy rain, hail, and snow with minimal damage or maintenance needs.
  • Energy Efficiency: Reflective properties that minimize heat absorption, potentially reducing cooling costs by 10-25% during hot Texas summers.
  • Fire Resistance: Non-combustible material with Class A fire rating, providing additional protection and potentially lower insurance premiums.
  • Environmental Benefits: 100% recyclable material with high recycled content, typically requires no tear-off of existing roofing, and reduces landfill waste.
  • Lightweight: Significantly lighter than tile or slate, reducing structural load and often eliminating the need for additional support.
  • Low Maintenance: Minimal upkeep requirements with simple periodic inspections and occasional cleaning to maintain optimal performance.
  • Impact Resistance: Superior resistance to damage from hail, falling branches, and debris compared to conventional roofing materials.
  • Insurance Advantages: Many insurance companies offer premium discounts for impact-resistant metal roofing due to its durability.
  • Increased Home Value: Metal roofing typically increases property value and enhances curb appeal with its modern, distinctive appearance.

Metal Roofing FAQs

How long do metal roofs last in Central Texas?

Quality metal roofing systems in Central Texas typically last for many decades with proper installation and minimal maintenance. Standing seam and stone coated steel systems, as well as other metal options often provide 60-75 years of service—approximately 5 times longer than traditional asphalt shingles. The exceptional longevity is a key factor in the overall value proposition despite the higher initial investment.

Are metal roofs noisy during rain and hailstorms?

This is a common misconception. Properly installed modern metal roofing with solid decking, underlayment, and insulation is no louder than other roofing materials during rainfall. The solid roof deck and attic space provide significant sound buffering. Some homeowners actually prefer the subtle ambient sound of light rain on their metal roof. During extreme weather like hail, metal roofs may be somewhat louder than other materials, but not to the disruptive extent often imagined.

How do metal roofs perform in severe weather and hailstorms?

Metal roofing excels in severe weather conditions common to Central Texas. Quality systems are rated for wind resistance up to 140+ mph, significantly higher than conventional roofing. While extremely large hail can cause cosmetic denting in some metal roofing, the structural integrity and water-shedding capabilities typically remain intact—unlike asphalt shingles which can lose granules and protective capacity. Many metal roofing systems carry Class 4 impact resistance ratings, the highest available.

What is the cost difference between metal roofing and traditional asphalt shingles?

Metal roofing typically costs 2-3 times more than standard asphalt shingles for initial installation, with standing seam and stone coated steel systems at the higher end and R panel and corrugated panels somewhat less. However, when considering the total lifecycle cost, metal roofing often proves more economical. A single metal roof can outlast 5 complete asphalt shingle replacements in Texas, offers energy savings throughout its lifespan, may qualify for insurance discounts, and can increase resale value—factors that offset the higher initial investment.

Can a metal roof be installed over my existing roof?

In many cases, yes, metal roofing can be installed over existing asphalt shingles, eliminating the time, expense, and landfill waste of tear-off. However, this depends on several factors including local building codes, the condition of the existing roof, and the type of metal roofing system being installed. We conduct a thorough assessment to determine if overlay installation is appropriate or if removal of the existing roofing is necessary for optimal results. Standing seam systems typically require tear-off, while some metal shingle systems are designed specifically for overlay applications.

Will a metal roof make my home hotter in summer?

Contrary to this common concern, quality metal roofing with reflective finishes actually helps keep homes cooler in hot weather. Metal roofs reflect solar radiation rather than absorbing it like darker asphalt shingles. Combined with proper ventilation and insulation, this reflective quality can reduce cooling costs by 10-25% during hot Texas summers. For maximum energy efficiency, lighter colors and specifically designed "cool roof" finishes offer the best heat reflection, though even darker metal roofs outperform asphalt shingles in energy efficiency.
